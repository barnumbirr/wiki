## Example allocations

`/64` IPv6 allocations are usually given to end users, who do not require any VLANs. It allows auto configuration, or SLAAC so makes life a lot easier when configuring.

```bash
2402:9400:1000:0::/64
2402:9400:1000:1::/64
2402:9400:1000:2::/64
2402:9400:1000:3::/64
2402:9400:1000:4::/64
2402:9400:1000:5::/64
2402:9400:1000:6::/64
2402:9400:1000:7::/64
2402:9400:1000:8::/64
2402:9400:1000:9::/64
2402:9400:1000:a::/64
2402:9400:1000:b::/64
2402:9400:1000:c::/64
2402:9400:1000:e::/64
2402:9400:1000:e::/64
2402:9400:1000:f::/64
2402:9400:1000:10::/64
2402:9400:1000:11::/64
```

`/48` allocations are usually provided to business, who require additional VLANs or may require the range to be split up.
